Digital Blackness in the Archive: A Documenting the Now Symposium

December 10-12, 2017 | #BlackDigArchive

The second Documenting the Now symposium, to be held in conjunction with our advisory board meeting, will address issues at the intersection on archival practice and the existence of Black people on the web and social media. Invited speakers and panelists will discuss their work on the Black experience in online spaces including Black joy and creativity, cultural and social expression, activism and resilience, state sponsored digital surveillance, inherent racism and bias in algorithm and platform design, digital humanities, and ethical data collection. The program will be an opportunity for the general public, activists, archivists, and the academic community, to learn and share in conversations about digital archives that center blackness. Because we too often attempt to find solutions before fully grasping the issues, an intentional goal of the program will be to broaden understanding of the realities faced by Black people participating in online spaces, in hopes that this knowledge will impact how archivists do their work around appraisal, collection, access, and long term preservation of social media and other web based content that document Black lives.
